§ 01 — The Problem
§ 01

Why Softmax fails under privacy

The motivation
Problem 01 · Training

LLMs memorize

Large language models trained on sensitive corpora can reproduce verbatim text from training data — exposing private information through membership inference and extraction attacks at inference time.

Problem 02 · Defence

DP-SGD is too costly

Per-sample gradient clipping scales poorly to billion-parameter models. Inference-time DP decoding — injecting noise into the token selection step — is far more practical, but has its own pitfalls.

Problem 03 · Mechanism

Softmax spreads the mass

The standard Exponential Mechanism distributes probability across all tokens. Under high privacy budgets, this assigns non-trivial mass to garbage tokens — producing incoherent, low-quality text.

§ 02 — Our Solution
§ 02

Our solution

Replace Gumbel with Exponential

RNM with Exponential Noise

Instead of Gumbel noise (Softmax sampling), we add i.i.d. Exponential noise to each logit and take the argmax. This is the Report-Noisy-Max (RNM) mechanism.

Exponential noise has a sharper one-sided decay than Gumbel. It strongly concentrates the winner on the highest-scoring tokens — fighting privacy-induced flattening.

yₜ = argmaxⱼ { ϕ̄ⱼ + Zⱼ },   Zⱼ ~ Exp(λ)
where  λ = 2Δ/ε = 4C/(B·ε)
RNM Exponential Noise ε-DP DClip sensitivity

Building on InvisibleInk

We directly extend Vinod et al. (2025), which achieves practical inference-time DP through two ideas:

DClip — clips only the difference between private and public logits, reducing sensitivity to Δ = 2C/B while preserving distributional structure.

Top-k⁺ decoding — restricts sampling to a safe subset derived from public logits, avoiding the unreliable vocabulary tail.

We keep both innovations and swap only the final sampling step: Gumbel-Max → RNM-Exponential. Same ε. Same framework. Strictly better.

§ 04 — Mathematical Foundation
§ 04

Mathematical foundation

Three supporting theorems
Theorem 1 · Equivalence

RNM-Exp ≡ Permute-and-Flip

Ding et al. (2021) proved that adding i.i.d. Exponential noise to scores and taking the argmax produces exactly the same output distribution as the Permute-and-Flip mechanism.

argmaxᵢ (qᵢ + Exp(λ)) ~ PF(q)

This lets us implement the simple RNM rule while inheriting all of PF's theoretical utility guarantees — including its known dominance over the Exponential Mechanism.

Theorem 2 · Privacy

Strict ε-DP under DClip

With DClip sensitivity Δ = 2C/B, calibrating the Exponential noise scale to λ = 2Δ/ε guarantees pure ε-differential privacy.

λ = 4C / (B · ε)

The proof follows a change-of-variables argument on the RNM probability integral, showing the likelihood ratio across adjacent datasets is bounded by eᵉ.

Theorem 3 · Utility

PF dominates EM — up to 2× better

For any privacy budget ε and any score distribution, the expected error of PF/RNM-Exp is always ≤ the expected error of the Exponential Mechanism.

E[E_PF] ≤ E[E_EM]  ∀ ε, q

In the asymptotic limit, the error ratio converges exactly to 2 — PF is twice as accurate as EM under the same privacy budget.
