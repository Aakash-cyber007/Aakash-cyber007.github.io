§ 02Our solution

Replace Gumbel with Exponential

Slide 03 / 08
Drop-in replacement
Core mechanism

RNM with Exponential noise

Instead of Gumbel noise (Softmax sampling), we add i.i.d. Exponential noise to each logit and take the argmax. This is the Report-Noisy-Max (RNM) mechanism.

Exponential noise has a sharper one-sided decay than Gumbel — strongly concentrating the winner on the highest-scoring tokens.

yt = argmaxj { ϕ̄j + Zj },  Zj ∼ Exp(λ)
where  λ = 2Δ/ε = 4C/(B · ε)
RNMExponential noiseε-DPDClip sensitivity
Built atop InvisibleInk · Vinod et al. 2025

A drop-in replacement

DClip — clips only the diff between private and public logits, reducing sensitivity to Δ = 2C/B while preserving structure.

Top-k⁺ decoding — restricts sampling to a safe subset derived from public logits, avoiding the unreliable vocabulary tail.

We keep both innovations and swap only the final sampling step: Gumbel-Max → RNM-Exponential. Same ε. Strictly better utility by Theorem 3.

§ 04Mathematical foundation

Three theorems, one conclusion

Slide 05 / 08
Theory
Theorem 1 · Equivalence

RNM-Exp ≡ Permute-and-Flip

Ding et al. (2021) proved that adding i.i.d. Exponential noise to scores and taking the argmax produces exactly the same output distribution as the Permute-and-Flip mechanism.

argmaxi (qi + Exp(λ)) ∼ PF(q)

This lets us implement the simple RNM rule while inheriting all of PF's utility guarantees — including its known dominance over the Exponential Mechanism.

Theorem 2 · Privacy

Strict ε-DP under DClip

With DClip sensitivity Δ = 2C/B, calibrating the Exponential noise scale to λ = 2Δ/ε guarantees pure ε-differential privacy.

λ = 4C / (B · ε)

The proof follows a change-of-variables argument on the RNM probability integral, showing the likelihood ratio across adjacent datasets is bounded by eε.

Theorem 3 · Utility

PF dominates EM up to 2×

For any privacy budget ε and any score distribution, the expected error of PF/RNM-Exp is always ≤ the expected error of the Exponential Mechanism.

𝔼[EPF] ≤ 𝔼[EEM]  ∀ ε, q

In the asymptotic limit, the error ratio converges exactly to 2. Empirically confirmed: 9/9 configs show RNM selects top-1 tokens more often.
